=== COMMENT 2 ===
A lot of learning in CSC 116 will happen online regardless of which section you take. The internet is literally built by programmers and a lot of them write down what they're doing. If online doesn't suit your learning style, go for one of the in-person instructors. But you'll definitely be doing some googling before it's over either way. As for lots of work and hard tests: welcome to any engineering major. Engineering is a hard field. There's some paths that are less hard, but none of them can really be described as easy.
=== END COMMENT 2 ===

=== COMMENT 13 ===
To succeed in CSC 116 with Dr. Schmidt: read the textbook — exams require full knowledge of Java principles and her videos are essentially just summaries of the textbook reading. Do as many Practice It problems as possible for tested material. Start projects as soon as they are released, don't procrastinate — Project 4 was the most time consuming. For labs, figure out the algorithm and design first and then program. Doing it in reverse leads to multiple bugs rooted in faulty logic. Get used to writing out on paper first how you would solve the problem. Go over the textbook along with Practice It before the semester starts — reading just 15 pages a day will finish it before classes begin.
=== END COMMENT 13 ===


=== COMMENT 14 ===
The best advice for CSC 116 and 216: start projects as early as possible. You never know what you'll run into and whether you'll need to wait on office hours or Piazza for a hint. If you wait until the last minute, everything must go right for you to finish on time.
=== END COMMENT 14 ===


=== COMMENT 15 ===
The last project in CSC 116 was an absolute beast. Every minute of free time I had was working on it for about a week. The other projects were manageable.
=== END COMMENT 15 ===


=== COMMENT 16 ===
If you have some coding experience, CSC 116 should be very easy. If you don't have any experience, it might take a few weeks or a month to get comfortable. For those who want to prepare, utilize the free LinkedIn Learning for students.
=== END COMMENT 16 ===


=== COMMENT 17 ===
Unlike high school, you don't get to skim through CSC 116 by having a basic understanding of coding logic. You need to read the textbook to answer the more Java-specific questions on tests. If you procrastinate on projects you're in trouble — a bad project grade is hard to recover from. Treat projects as if they were the class final. You should never plan on finishing them the night before they are due because you will run into some last-minute problem that takes hours to fix, as is the nature of programming.
=== END COMMENT 17 ===
